## LANDING COPY PROOFREAD

Only strings with a real grammar, clarity, or consistency problem are listed. Items marked "(optional)" are taste, not correctness. Brand voice and hard rules are preserved in every suggestion.

**[mirror] (the flagged section)** — CURRENT: "By everything anyone can see, you are doing it all right." -> SUGGESTED: "As far as anyone can see, you are doing everything right."
Reason: "By everything anyone can see" is not idiomatic English and is the line that makes the paragraph read awkwardly. "As far as anyone can see" is the natural phrasing. This is the main grammar problem in the flagged section; the headline itself ("On the outside... On the inside...") is correct and can stay.

**[hero] subhead** — CURRENT: "...because nobody looked for the real cause yet." -> SUGGESTED: "...because nobody has looked for the real cause yet."
Reason: "yet" needs the present perfect ("has looked") to match "Nobody has told you" earlier in the same sentence. Current tense mix reads slightly off.

**[faq] "Will this work with my Hashimoto's..."** — CURRENT: "Divya and Shivani both started here. You read what changed for them." -> SUGGESTED: "Divya started exactly here. You can read what changed for her above."
Reason: two problems. (1) Consistency: the proof section shows Divya, Upasana, Grishma, and Amoolya. There is no Shivani anywhere on the page, so "You read what changed for them" points at a story the reader cannot find. (2) Tense: "You read what changed" should be "You can read what changed". If Shivani is meant to appear in the proof section, add her there instead of removing her here.

**[why] section markers foot** — CURRENT: "until you start feeling forty five when you are only thirty two." -> SUGGESTED: "until you start feeling forty-five when you are only thirty-two." (optional)
Reason: compound numbers are hyphenated when written as words. Hyphens are allowed under the no-em-dash rule.

**[why] deck** — CURRENT: "So you got a medicine for the symptom, while the real causes kept growing quietly." -> SUGGESTED: "So you were given medicine for the symptom, while the real causes kept growing quietly." (optional)
Reason: "a medicine" reads slightly off with an uncountable noun. "were given medicine" is cleaner and keeps the calm clinical voice. Acceptable as-is in colloquial Indian English, hence optional.

**[offer] price reframe** — CURRENT: "It keeps this for women who are genuinely ready to fix this and not just collecting more opinions." -> SUGGESTED: "It keeps this space for women who are genuinely ready to fix this, not just collect more opinions." (optional)
Reason: "keeps this for" is vague (this what?), and "ready to fix this and not just collecting" mixes an infinitive with an "-ing" form. The suggestion makes the parallel clean ("ready to fix this, not just collect"). Optional because the meaning still comes through.
