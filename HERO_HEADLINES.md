# FitWithPalak Clinical Track VSL, Hero Headline Options

Verdict: The "reports say normal, body says otherwise" angle wins for a cold thyroid lead. It names the WHO, states the exact contradiction she lives with, and relieves blame in one breath, before she can ask "is this me?"

Note on letters: the hero carries **N** (name the who) and **R** (relieve the blame) as its primary jobs. **B** (30-minute dated outcome), **I** (proof), **E** (erase the four costs) and **Rush + Reassure** are reserved for later sections and the CTA, not this line.

---

### 1. Best, the "normal reports" tension

Your reports say *normal*. Your body says otherwise.
There is a real cause, and it can be found.

Angle: **N + R**, Schwartz tension. Leads with the exact contradiction she cannot explain, then externalises it onto a missing cause instead of her, so she feels seen without feeling blamed.

---

### 2. Still tired despite the medicine

You take the thyroid pill every single day.
And you still wake up *tired*, bloated, and stuck.

Angle: **N**, Collier inner voice. This is her literal daily reality in her own words, which pulls the right reader in and lets the wrong one leave.

---

### 3. Future-pace the answer

On thyroid medication and still feeling this way?
In thirty minutes, you can finally know *why*.

Angle: **N + B**, PAS solution turn. Names the who, then paces the relief of an answer with a concrete clock, without promising a cure.

---

### 4. The rising dose

The dose keeps *climbing*. The tiredness never leaves.
This was never about how hard you were trying.

Angle: **R**, relieve the blame. Hits the frustration of an ever-rising dose, then lifts the fault off her completely.

---

### 5. Call out the woman

For the woman on thyroid medication whose blood work
reads *fine*, while she feels anything but.

Angle: **N**, Kennedy message-to-market match. A direct call-out that makes the exact reader feel caught, though it opens softer than the pain-first lines above.

---

Locked to the rules: no em dashes, no use of "free", no reader blame, plain Indian English, no metaphor, no invented claim or cure. All five stay specific to being on thyroid medication and still feeling unwell.
